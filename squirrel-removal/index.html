Signs You Might Need Squirrel Removal Services in New Brunswick

Don't underestimate the potential for damage and disruption that squirrels can cause. Recognizing the early signs of a squirrelwildlife infestation on your New Brunswick property is crucial for swift action. Ignoring these indicators can lead to more significant and costly problems down the line. Here are some common signs that suggest you might need professional squirrel removal assistance:

Noises in Your Attic or Walls

One of the most common giveaways is the presence of unusual sounds coming from within your home's structure. Squirrels are active during the day, especially in the early morning and late afternoon. You might hear scratching, scurrying, chattering, or gnawing sounds. These noises can indicate squirrels moving around, building nests, or chewing on materials within your walls or attic space. The sounds can be particularly unsettling and disrupt your sleep or create anxiety.

Visible Damage to Your Home's Exterior

Squirrels need to find a way into your home, and they are incredibly persistent. Look for signs of damage to the exterior of your house, such as chewed or gnawed wood around entry points like eaves, fascia boards, soffits, or window frames. They may also damage vents, shutters, or even roofing materials. These visible signs are a clear indication of their attempts to gain access.

Damaged or Displaced Insulation

Attics provide squirrels with a relatively safe and secluded environment to build nests and raise their young. If you notice compressed, torn, or displaced insulation in your attic, it could be a sign of squirrel activity. They use insulation material for nesting, and their movements can disturb and damage it, reducing its effectiveness and potentially leading to higher energy bills.

Chewed Electrical Wires or Plumbing

This is perhaps one of the most dangerous consequences of a squirrel infestation. Squirrels have a natural instinct to chew to keep their teeth from growing too long. Unfortunately, they often target electrical wires or plumbing pipes. Chewed electrical wires pose a significant fire hazard, while damaged pipes can lead to costly water damage. Discovering any signs of rodent gnawing on these critical systems requires immediate attention.

Finding Squirrel Droppings or Urine

Like any animal, squirrels leave behind waste. Finding squirrel droppings or noticing a strong, unpleasant odor, particularly in enclosed spaces like attics or garages, can be a sign of an active infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically small and pellet-shaped. Their urine can also permeate materials and create a persistent odor that is difficult to eliminate without professional cleaning.

Seeing Squirrels Entering or Exiting Your Home

The most undeniable sign is observing squirrels entering or exiting your home through identified openings. Pay attention to their behavior around your roofline, eaves, or other potential entry points. If you see them repeatedly using a specific spot to access your home, it's highly likely they have established a den inside.

Damaged Bird Feeders or Garden Items

While not a direct sign of an indoor infestation, increased squirrel activity around your yard, such as consistently raided bird feeders or damaged garden plants, can sometimes precede or coincide with them seeking shelter indoors, especially as weather changes or food sources become scarce outside. If you're noticing an unusual number of squirrels in your yard, it's wise to be vigilant for potential indoor issues.

Presence of Squirrel Nests

Squirrels build nests using materials they find, both indoors and outdoors. Finding a collection of leaves, twigs, insulation, or other soft materials in your attic, eaves, or other hidden areas is a strong indicator of their presence. These nests often house female squirrels and their young.

Recognizing these signs early can save you a lot of trouble, expense, and potential danger. If you suspect you have a squirrel problem in New Brunswick, NJ, don't delay in seeking professional help. Understanding Squirrel Behavior and Why They Invade Homes

To effectively deal with a squirrel problem, it helps to understand a bit about their behavior and what drives them to seek shelter in human structures. Squirrels are small, agile rodents that are prevalent throughout New Brunswick and the surrounding areas, including East Brunswick and Piscataway. While they play a role in the ecosystem, their adaptive nature means they are quite capable of living in close proximity to humans, and sometimes that proximity becomes too close for comfort.

Seasonal Influences on Squirrel Activity

Squirrel activity and their likelihood of seeking indoor shelter often correlate with the seasons. As the weather cools in the fall, squirrels become more focused on gathering and storing food for the winter. During this time, they may also begin to look for suitable overwintering locations, and your warm, sheltered attic can seem like an ideal spot. In the late winter and early spring, female squirrels often look for safe, protected places to build nests and give birth to their young. An attic or wall void provides a safe haven from predators and harsh weather conditions.

Squirrels and Their Diet

Squirrels are primarily herbivores, feeding on nuts, seeds, fruits, and buds. However, they are opportunistic eaters and will also consume insects, bird eggs, and even garbage. Their constant search for food can bring them into residential areas, where bird feeders, gardens, and unsecured trash cans provide easy access to meals. This constant foraging can bring them closer to your home and increase the likelihood of them exploring potential entry points.

The Need for Shelter

Beyond food, squirrels need shelter to protect themselves from predators and the elements. Trees are their primary habitat, but when natural shelter is limited or compromised, human structures become attractive alternatives. Attics, chimneys, sheds, and even vehicles can provide the warmth and protection they seek, especially during cold winters or hot summers.

Squirrel Prolificacy

Squirrels can reproduce quickly. Females typically have two litters per year, one in late winter/early spring and another in mid-to-late summer. A litter can consist of anywhere from 2 to 8 young. This means a small initial problem can quickly escalate into a larger infestation if not addressed promptly. The presence of young squirrels also requires a different approach to removal, as humane methods are crucial to avoid separating mothers from their offspring.

Squirrels' Gnawing Behavior

As mentioned before, squirrels have continuously growing incisor teeth, and they must chew to wear them down. This is a natural and necessary behavior for them. Unfortunately, this gnawing extends to various materials, including wood, plastic, wiring, and even metal. This is why they can cause so much damage when they gain access to your home's structure.

Understanding these aspects of squirrel behavior helps illuminate why they might be targeting your property. Recognizing their needs and motivations can aid in implementing effective prevention strategies alongside professional removal services. Preventing Future Squirrel Infestations in New Brunswick

While expert squirrel removal is essential for resolving an existing problem, taking proactive steps to prevent future infestations is equally important. Once we've safely removed the squirrels from your New Brunswick, NJ property, we'll provide you with valuable advice on how to make your home less attractive to these furry visitors. Prevention is often the most cost-effective and long-term solution.

Seal Up Potential Entry Points

This is the most critical step in preventing squirrels from re-entering your home. As we discussed, squirrels are adept at finding small openings. Thoroughly inspect your home's exterior and seal any gaps, cracks, or holes you find. Pay close attention to:

Roof and Eaves

Check for damaged or missing shingles, loose or rotten fascia boards, and gaps where the roof meets the wall. Install chimney caps and screening over vents (ensure proper ventilation is maintained) to prevent entry. Trim overhanging branches that provide easy access to your roof.

Vents and Pipes

Ensure all attic vents, dryer vents, and plumbing vents are properly screened and in good repair. Squirrels can easily chew through plastic vents, so consider upgrading to more durable metal materials if necessary.

Windows and Doors

Repair any damaged window screens or door seals. While less common entry points than the attic or roof, squirrels can exploit weaknesses in these areas, especially if they are damaged.

Siding and Foundation

Inspect your siding for any holes or gaps, particularly where different materials meet. Check the foundation for cracks or openings that could allow squirrels or other rodents to gain access.

Trim Trees and Bushes

Trees and bushes that are close to your home's roofline or siding provide squirrels with easy access points. Trim branches back at least 6-8 feet from your home to eliminate a direct pathway. Keep bushes and shrubs well-trimmed and away from the foundation to reduce potential hiding spots.

Secure Food Sources

Eliminate attractants that draw squirrels to your property in the first place. This includes:

Bird Feeders

Consider using squirrel-proof feeders or placing feeders on poles away from structures. Clean up spilled seed regularly.

Garbage Cans

Use sturdy garbage cans with tight-fitting lids. Consider storing garbage cans in a shed or garage until pickup day.

Gardens and Fruit Trees

Harvest fruits and vegetables promptly. Consider fencing or netting to protect garden beds from foraging squirrels.

For comprehensive and effective exclusion, consider professional services offered by New Brunswick Pest Pros. We can identify and seal potential entry points you may have overlooked and ensure the work is done correctly and securely. Our expertise can save you time, effort, and the frustration of future infestations.

Regular Property Maintenance

Routine maintenance of your home's exterior is crucial. Regularly inspect your roof, siding, vents, and other potential entry points for any signs of wear or damage. Addressing minor issues promptly can prevent them from becoming major pathways for squirrels.

Educational Awareness

Educate yourself and your family about squirrel behavior and the importance of not feeding them. While it may seem harmless, providing food can make them more comfortable around your home and increase the likelihood of them seeking shelter indoors.

Professional Consultations

If you're unsure about potential entry points or effective prevention methods, consult with wildlife control professionals. The Dangers and Risks Associated with Squirrel Infestations

Beyond the annoying noises and general nuisance, a squirrel infestation can pose several serious risks to both your property and your health. It's important to be aware of these potential dangers, which is why prompt and effective squirrel removal in New Brunswick, NJ is so important. Ignoring a squirrel problem can lead to significant issues that are far more costly and difficult to resolve than addressing the initial infestation.

Fire Hazards from Chewed Wires

As mentioned earlier, squirrels have a strong instinct to gnaw, and electrical wiring is often a target. Chewed insulation on wires exposes the conductors, creating a significant risk of short circuits, sparks, and ultimately, house fires. This is one of the most alarming dangers associated with squirrel infestations and requires immediate attention from both a wildlife professional and potentially an electrician to assess and repair any damaged wiring.

Damage to Structural Components

Squirrels can cause extensive damage to the structural elements of your home. They may chew on wooden beams, support structures, and even compromise the integrity of your roof or siding as they create or enlarge entry points. This damage can be costly to repair and, in severe cases, can impact the safety and stability of your home.

Damage to Insulation and Energy Efficiency

Squirrels use insulation for nesting material, tearing and displacing it. This damage can significantly reduce the effectiveness of your home's insulation, leading to higher energy bills as your HVAC system works harder to maintain a consistent temperature. Moreover, squirrel droppings and urine can contaminate insulation, requiring its removal and replacement for health reasons.

Contamination and Health Risks

Squirrels, like many wildlife animals, can carry diseases, parasites, and bacteria. Their droppings and urine can contaminate surfaces and air quality, posing health risks to humans, particularly those with respiratory sensitivities. Hantavirus, leptospirosis, and salmonella are among the potential diseases associated with rodent and wildlife infestations. Furthermore, squirrels can bring fleas, ticks, and mites into your home, which can then infest your pets and family. Diseases like Lyme disease or Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever can be transmitted through tick bites, and some tick species are carried by squirrels.

Odors and Unpleasant Smells

The accumulation of squirrel droppings and urine can create strong, unpleasant odors within your home, particularly in enclosed spaces like attics or wall voids. These odors can be difficult to eliminate and can permeate porous materials, making your home environment uncomfortable and potentially unhealthy. Deceased squirrels that are not located and removed can also create a very strong and persistent odor.

Damage to Belongings

Beyond structural damage, squirrels can also damage personal belongings stored in attics or other areas they inhabit. They may chew on stored items, clothing, or even valuable documents when building nests or foraging for materials.

Disruption and Stress

The constant noise, worry about damage, and potential health risks associated with a squirrel infestation can cause significant stress and disruption to your daily life. Knowing that there are wild animals living within your home can be unsettling and lead to lost sleep and anxiety.

Considering these potential dangers, it's clear that a squirrel infestation is not a problem to be taken lightly.
